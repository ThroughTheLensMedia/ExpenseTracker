**Title**: The Photographer’s Guide to Audit-Proofing Your Business in 2026
**Read Time**: 6 Minutes

### The Problem: The "Shoebox" Method
Let’s be honest. Most of us start our photography businesses with a shoebox full of receipts and a "we'll figure it out in April" attitude. But once you start buying $2,000 lenses and driving 100 miles to a wedding venue, the shoebox method becomes a liability. The IRS doesn't care how good your bokeh is—they care about your documentation. 

### Enter the "Rocket Money" Era
The biggest headache for freelancers is separating personal and business spending. We all buy coffee on the way to a shoot. We all buy "props" that occasionally end up in our own living rooms. 

Instead of manually typing every transaction into a spreadsheet, the modern way is to leverage what you already have. By using **Rocket Money** to track your banking and importing that data into a specialized **Studio Tracker**, you eliminate 90% of the manual labor. 

### 3 Columns of a Healthy Photo Business

#### 1. Depreciation (The Hidden Goldmine)
Equipment isn't just an expense; it's an asset. Under Section 179, photographers can often deduct the entire purchase price of gear in the first year. The Studio Tracker automates this calculation, moving the value from your "Equipment" tab directly to your Schedule C Tax report.

#### 2. The 67¢ Per Mile Rule
In 2026, every mile matters. If you're driving to client meetings or scouting locations, you're leaving money on the table if you aren't logging it. Integrated trackers that pull the official IRS rates mean you never have to guess the math again.

#### 3. Receipt Integrity
If an expense is over $75, you need a receipt. It's that simple. A digital dashboard that flags missing receipts with a red "Needed" badge ensures you never get caught off guard in an audit. 

### Conclusion
You didn't become a photographer to become an accountant. But to keep being a photographer, you HAVE to manage your finances. Stop using software that was built for "big companies" and start using a tool built for the creative grind.
